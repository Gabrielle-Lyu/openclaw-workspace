### The Three Pillars of the 2026 Rotation

**Pillar 1: Valuation Mean Reversion**

*Thesis:* Three years of mega-cap outperformance (2023-2025) created historically extreme valuation gaps comparable only to late-1990s tech bubble.

*Evidence:*
- S&P 500 cap-weighted +86% vs. Equal Weight +43% (2023-2025) = 43 percentage points
- This gap only seen once before: late-1990s
- Performance driven by multiple expansion, not superior earnings growth

*Mechanism:* When growth expectations moderate (AI monetization timelines extend, competition increases, regulation looms), high multiples compress. Lower-multiple value stocks become relatively attractive.

*Outcome:* Russell 2000 companies trading at discount multiples + accelerating earnings (19% growth) = powerful rerating catalyst.

---

**Pillar 2: Monetary Policy Transmission with Lag**

*Thesis:* Fed rate cuts in 2025 (75bps) are now transmitting through to rate-sensitive sectors with characteristic 6-9 month lag.

*Evidence:*
- Small caps traditionally benefit most from lower rates (higher debt burdens, refinancing sensitivity)
- Regional bank lending conditions improving
- Term structure flattening = reduced financing costs for growth capex

*Mechanism:* Lower policy rates → reduced corporate borrowing costs → improved small-cap profitability → multiple expansion. Simultaneously, lower rates reduce discount rate for DCF models, benefiting longer-duration cash flows.

*Outcome:* "Small-Cap Renaissance" as Russell 2000 breaks 13-quarter underperformance streak.

---

**Pillar 3: Domestic Policy Shift ("Trump Trade 2.0")**

*Thesis:* Policy focus on domestic manufacturing, deregulation, and fiscal stimulus disproportionately benefits small-cap, domestically-focused companies.

*Evidence:*
- Energy sector deregulation supporting +21% performance
- Industrial policy favoring domestic production (materials +17%)
- Tax policy expectations supporting buyback/dividend capacity

*Mechanism:* Large-cap multinationals face geopolitical headwinds (China tensions, Europe slowdown). Small-caps with domestic revenue exposure avoid these drags while benefiting from pro-business domestic policy.

*Outcome:* Sector rotation into industrials, energy, materials—classic "tangible economy" plays.
